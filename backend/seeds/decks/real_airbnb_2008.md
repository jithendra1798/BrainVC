# AirBed&Breakfast — Book rooms with locals, rather than hotels

Web platform where users rent out their space to travelers. Pre-seed. San Francisco, US. (2008)

---

## Problem

Price is an important concern for customers booking travel online.
Hotels leave you disconnected from the city and its culture.
No easy way exists to book a room with a local or become a host.

---

## Solution

A web platform where users can rent out their space to travelers:
travelers save money, hosts make money, and both share culture.

---

## Market Validation

Couchsurfing.com has over 630,000 registered users.
Craigslist has 17,000 temporary housing listings per week across
San Francisco and New York City alone.

---

## Market Size

1.9 billion+ trips booked worldwide per year (total available market).
532 million budget and online trips (serviceable market).
Target: 10.6 million trips booked with AirBed&Breakfast.

---

## Business Model

We take a 10% commission on each transaction.
At a $70 average nightly fee, that is $25 average fee per transaction.
Projected revenue: $200M per year by 2011 on 10.6M trips.

---

## Market Adoption

Launch at events with scarce hotel supply: the DNC and other conventions.
Dual-post listings to Craigslist to bootstrap supply and demand.
Partnerships with cheap and alternative travel sites.

---

## Competition

Couchsurfing (free, no transaction), Craigslist (unsafe, no trust),
Hotels.com and Hostels.com (expensive, impersonal). AirBed&Breakfast is
the first transaction-based temporary housing marketplace.

---

## Competitive Advantages

First to market for transaction-based stays. Hosts have a financial
incentive to list. List once and it stays live. Trust through profiles.
Ease of use. Design-driven experience.
