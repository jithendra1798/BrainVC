# UberCab — Next-Generation Car Service

On-demand luxury car service with 1-click hailing. Pre-seed. San Francisco, US. (2008)

---

## Problem

Cab companies run on decades-old radio dispatch technology with no
digital hail. Taxi medallion monopolies reduce service quality and
accountability. Riders face long waits; drivers sit idle between fares
and pay high costs to enter the industry.

---

## Solution

A fast and efficient on-demand car service for business professionals:
members-only luxury fleet, 1-click hailing from a phone, GPS-based
matching, and pickup within 5-10 minutes in covered zones. Launching in
San Francisco and New York first.

---

## Technology

GPS coordination between riders and drivers. Demand prediction
positions vehicles optimally by time of day, weather, and traffic, so
cars are near future demand before it happens.

---

## Market

Americans spend roughly $4.2 billion per year on taxi and limousine
services. Smartphone adoption is accelerating rapidly as of 2008,
making location-aware mobile services viable for the first time.

---

## Scenarios

Best case: UberCab becomes the market leader in on-demand transport and
generates $1B+ in annual revenue.
Realistic case: UberCab becomes the premium car service in the top 5 US
cities, generating $20-30M in annual profit.
Worst case: UberCab remains a small luxury car service in San Francisco.

---

## Progress to Date

UberCab.com domain secured, company incorporated, working prototype
built and demonstrated. First converted town car operating in San
Francisco.
